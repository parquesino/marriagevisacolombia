Our Detailed Legal Process

1. Immigration Strategy & Planning

We create a robust legal strategy for your case and plan each step clearly and responsibly, including travel arrangements.

2. Document Collection & Review

An expert lawyer reviews your documents and form answers to ensure they meet all visa requirements and align with the designed strategy.

3. Submission to the Ministry

We prepare, organize, and submit your complete application through the official SITAC system of the Colombian Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

4. Follow-Up & Requirements

We monitor your case and diligently respond to any request for additional information that the immigration authority may require.

5. Approved Visa Registration

Once approved, we handle the mandatory registration of your visa for you in the Migración Colombia system to activate it.

6. Foreigner's ID Appointment

We request and schedule your appointment at Migración Colombia for you to obtain your Cédula de Extranjería (Foreigner's ID).

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between an 'Inadmissible' and 'Rejected' visa?

This is a crucial distinction. An **Inadmissible** visa means the authority requires more information or a document correction; it is not a final denial. In this case, our service includes resubmitting the application as many times as needed at no extra charge for our legal fees. A **Rejected** visa is a final, discretionary decision. In this case, our service is considered complete, and reapplying would require a new service agreement after a 6-month waiting period.

How long does the entire process take?

Once all documents are gathered and the application is submitted, the review period by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs is typically 3 to 5 weeks. Document collection can add additional time to this timeline.
